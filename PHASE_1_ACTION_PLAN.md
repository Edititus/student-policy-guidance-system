### **Week 6: Interview Protocol Finalization**

#### Administrator Interview Protocol

**Target Participants:** 10-15 staff members
- Registrars / Deputy Registrars (2-3)
- Student Affairs Officers (3-4)
- Academic Planning Officers (2-3)
- ICT/Help Desk Staff (2-3)
- Faculty Advisors (1-2)

**Interview Structure (45-60 minutes):**

**Section 1: Current State (15 min)**
1. What are the most common policy-related questions from students?
2. How do students currently seek policy information?
3. What challenges do you face in responding to policy queries?
4. How many policy inquiries do you handle per day/week?
5. What percentage of queries are repetitive?

**Section 2: Pain Points (15 min)**
6. What policy areas cause the most confusion?
7. Are there policies that are frequently misinterpreted?
8. How do you handle ambiguous or complex policy questions?
9. What resources do students use (handbooks, website, etc.)?
10. How accurate/up-to-date are current information sources?

**Section 3: AI System Expectations (15 min)**
11. What would an ideal policy guidance system look like?
12. What features would be most valuable?
13. What concerns do you have about an AI system?
14. How should the system handle uncertain/ambiguous queries?
15. What role should human oversight play?

**Section 4: Implementation Considerations (10 min)**
16. What policies should be included first (priority areas)?
17. How should the system integrate with existing systems?
18. What training/support would staff need?
19. How should system accuracy be evaluated?

#### Student Interview Protocol

**Target Participants:** 15-20 students
- Mix of years (100-400 level)
- Different programs (Sciences, Arts, Engineering, etc.)
- Both genders
- International and local students

**Focus Group Structure (60-75 minutes, 4-5 students per group):**

**Section 1: Current Information Seeking Behavior (20 min)**
1. Where do you go when you have questions about university policies?
2. Have you ever struggled to find policy information? Examples?
3. How satisfied are you with current information sources?
4. How long does it typically take to get answers?
5. Have you ever received incorrect or conflicting information?

**Section 2: Specific Policy Areas (20 min)**
6. What policy areas have you needed information about?
   - Registration procedures
   - Course deferment/withdrawal
   - Academic probation/appeals
   - Financial aid/scholarships
   - Exam/assessment regulations
   - Student conduct/discipline
7. Which areas were hardest to understand?
8. Can you describe a frustrating policy-related experience?

**Section 3: Technology & AI Perceptions (15 min)**
9. Have you used chatbots before? (e.g., customer service, ChatGPT)
10. How comfortable are you with AI providing policy guidance?
11. Would you trust an AI system for policy information?
12. What concerns would you have?
13. When would you prefer human assistance over AI?

**Section 4: Design Preferences (15 min)**
14. What features would make a policy guidance system useful?
15. How should answers be presented? (text, examples, step-by-step)
16. Should the system show sources/policy references?
17. What if the system isn't sure of an answer?
18. Would you use voice input or prefer typing?

**Interview Best Practices:**
- Arrive 15 minutes early to set up
- Test audio equipment before each session
- Create rapport with small talk
- Use probing questions ("Can you tell me more about that?")
- Watch for non-verbal cues
- Take brief notes during (full notes after)
- Save recordings with consistent naming: `Admin_01_2026-02-XX.m4a`

**Data Management:**
- [ ] Store recordings in secure, encrypted folder
- [ ] Transcribe within 24-48 hours while fresh
- [ ] Anonymize transcripts (replace names with codes: A01, S01, etc.)
- [ ] Backup all files to cloud storage (encrypted)

**Step 3: Rule Extraction Template**
For each policy section, identify:

```markdown
## Policy: Course Registration Deadlines

**Category:** Academic > Registration
**Source:** Student Handbook 2024, Page 15

**Rule:**
IF [student is returning] AND [registration period is open]
THEN [student must register within 2 weeks of semester start]

**Condition Details:**
- Registration period: First 2 weeks of semester
- Applies to: Continuing students (not freshmen)

**Action:**
- Student completes online registration
- Pays registration fees

**Exception:**
IF [student has documented medical reason]
THEN [may register up to 4 weeks with approval]
- Requires: Medical certificate + Dean's approval

**Consequence (if violated):**
- Late registration fee: ₦5,000
- After 4 weeks: Cannot register (must defer)

**Ambiguities:**
- What counts as "documented medical reason"?
- Who approves exceptions (Dean vs. Registrar)?

**Related Policies:**
- Payment schedules (Financial Policy #3)
- Deferment procedures (Admin Policy #12)
```

#### Requirements Specification Document

**Functional Requirements:**

**FR1: Query Processing**
- FR1.1: System shall accept policy queries in natural language
- FR1.2: System shall support both text and voice input
- FR1.3: System shall handle follow-up questions with context

**FR2: Policy Retrieval**
- FR2.1: System shall retrieve relevant policies from knowledge base
- FR2.2: System shall rank policies by relevance
- FR2.3: System shall handle queries spanning multiple policy areas

**FR3: Response Generation**
- FR3.1: System shall generate clear, concise answers
- FR3.2: System shall provide step-by-step guidance when applicable
- FR3.3: System shall cite specific policy sources
- FR3.4: System shall indicate confidence level (High/Medium/Low)

**FR4: Rule-Based Reasoning**
- FR4.1: System shall apply IF-THEN policy rules
- FR4.2: System shall consider student context (program, year, status)
- FR4.3: System shall handle policy exceptions
- FR4.4: System shall explain reasoning chain

**FR5: Human-in-the-Loop**
- FR5.1: System shall escalate uncertain queries to admins
- FR5.2: Admins shall review and override AI responses
- FR5.3: System shall log all escalations and overrides
- FR5.4: System shall learn from admin corrections

**FR6: Admin Dashboard**
- FR6.1: Admins shall view recent queries and responses
- FR6.2: Admins shall update knowledge base
- FR6.3: Admins shall view analytics (query trends, accuracy metrics)

**Non-Functional Requirements:**

**NFR1: Performance**
- Response time: <3 seconds for 90% of queries
- Support 100+ concurrent users
- 99% uptime during business hours

**NFR2: Accuracy**
- >85% accuracy on policy information
- <10% escalation rate

**NFR3: Security**
- End-to-end encryption for user data
- GDPR/NDPR compliance
- Role-based access control

**NFR4: Usability**
- WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility
- Mobile responsive
- Multi-language support (English, Hausa, Yoruba, Igbo)

**NFR5: Maintainability**
- Admins can update policies without developer help
- Modular architecture for easy updates

## 🚨 Common Challenges & Solutions

### Challenge 1: Ethical Clearance Delays
**Problem:** IRB approval takes 4-6 weeks instead of 2-4  
**Solution:**
- Submit application early (Week 5)
- Follow up weekly with ethics committee
- Use waiting time for policy collection and protocol refinement
- Conduct pilot interviews with colleagues (for practice)

### Challenge 2: Participant Recruitment Difficulties
**Problem:** Can't get enough admin staff or students  
**Solution:**
- Offer convenient interview times (lunch break, after work)
- Provide incentives (certificates, refreshments, research credit)
- Use snowball sampling (ask participants to refer others)
- Reduce sample size if saturation achieved early (quality > quantity)

### Challenge 3: Partner University Access
**Problem:** Universities slow to respond or deny access  
**Solution:**
- Focus on Veritas University as primary site
- Use publicly available policy documents (from websites)
- Leverage supervisor/department connections for introductions
- Offer mutual benefit (share findings, free system access)

### Challenge 4: Time Management
**Problem:** Interviews take longer than expected  
**Solution:**
- Block full days for data collection (not spread out)
- Hire transcription service (if budget allows)
- Use speech-to-text tools (Otter.ai, Google Docs voice typing)
- Start analysis while still collecting (don't wait till end)

### Challenge 5: Data Analysis Overwhelm
**Problem:** 25+ hours of interview audio is daunting  
**Solution:**
- Transcribe incrementally (don't wait till all done)
- Use qualitative analysis software (free: Taguette, QualCoder)
- Focus on research questions (don't code everything)
- Work with a peer for inter-coder reliability
